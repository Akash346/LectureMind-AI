# Code of Conduct

LectureMind-AI is an open, educational project. We want participation to be a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

This Code of Conduct is adapted in spirit from the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org), v2.1, and applies to all project spaces — the repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other official channel — and when an individual is representing the project in public spaces.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, considerate, and welcoming, especially to newcomers and students.
- Assume good faith and give constructive, actionable feedback.
- Accept feedback gracefully and focus on what is best for the community and the project.
- Use inclusive language and respect differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Credit others' work and contributions.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form.
- Sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Publishing others' private information (such as a physical or email address) without explicit permission.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, reviews, or community spaces.
- Any other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it privately to the maintainer:

- **Akash Nallagonda** — **akashnallagonda9@gmail.com**

Reports will be handled discreetly. Please include what happened, where, when, and any context or evidence that helps. You will not be penalized for reporting in good faith.

## Enforcement

The maintainer is responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct and will respond to reports fairly and consistently. Depending on severity, responses may include:

1. **Correction** — a private warning and clarification of the violation.
2. **Warning** — a formal warning with consequences for continued behavior.
3. **Temporary ban** — a temporary block from interaction with the project.
4. **Permanent ban** — a permanent ban from the community for severe or repeated violations.

The maintainer may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons when appropriate.
